Vendor onboarding must include dual approval from procurement and finance teams before activation in the system.
Any modification to vendor bank account details requires maker-checker validation and documented approval.
All vendor changes must be logged with timestamp, user ID, and approval reference.

Access to financial systems must follow least privilege principles and be approved by system owners.
Quarterly access reviews must be performed and documented for all critical applications.
Privileged access must be reviewed monthly and monitored for unusual activity.

Segregation of duties must ensure that no individual has end-to-end control over financial transactions.
Users responsible for vendor creation must not have rights to approve payments.
System roles must be reviewed periodically to ensure no SoD conflicts exist.

All approvals must be documented and retained for audit for a minimum of 7 years.
Electronic approvals must include audit trails such as timestamps and approver identity.
Manual approvals must be scanned and stored in a centralized repository.

Payment processing must include validation controls to prevent duplicate or fraudulent transactions.
Any payment exceeding threshold limits must require additional approval from senior management.
Automated alerts must be triggered for high-risk transactions.

Change management procedures must include approval, testing, and rollback planning before deployment.
All production changes must be logged and reviewed periodically.
Emergency changes must be documented and approved retrospectively.

Data retention policies must ensure financial records are preserved securely and retrievable for audits.
Sensitive data must be encrypted both in transit and at rest.
Access to sensitive financial data must be restricted and monitored.

Incident management processes must ensure all financial anomalies are reported and investigated.
Root cause analysis must be documented for all major incidents.
Corrective actions must be tracked and validated.

Vendor risk assessments must be conducted prior to onboarding and periodically thereafter.
High-risk vendors must undergo enhanced due diligence.
Contracts must include clauses for compliance and audit rights.

User provisioning must require formal request, approval, and validation before access is granted.
Deprovisioning must occur immediately upon termination or role change.
Inactive accounts must be disabled after 30 days.

Audit logs must be maintained for all critical system activities.
Logs must be reviewed periodically for suspicious activity.
Log retention must comply with regulatory requirements.

Backup and recovery procedures must ensure data integrity and availability.
Periodic disaster recovery testing must be conducted.
Recovery objectives must be defined and validated.
